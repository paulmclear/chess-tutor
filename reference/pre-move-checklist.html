The Pre-Move Checklist

Run this before every move. You play classical — you have the time. Use it.

1
What did their last move do? Every move has a point. Does it attack a piece, threaten a capture, or set up check/mate?
2
Scan for loose pieces — both sides. Anything attacked & under-defended? Theirs = free material. Mine = emergency.
3
Pick my move — then re-check it. Played in my head: does it hang anything? Walk into a check or fork? This kills most blunders.
4
All checks & captures. Mine and theirs. Forcing moves are where tactics — and disasters — live.
“Their threat? · What’s loose? · Does my move hang? · Checks & captures?”

Loose piece = attacked more times than it’s defended (or defended by something more valuable than the attacker). Loose pieces drop. That’s most of chess below 1300.
