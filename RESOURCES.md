# Chess Improvement Resources

Curated, high-trust sources for an ~800 → 1200 player who plays classical chess on
chess.com. The guiding principle (confirmed across every source below): **below
~1300, games are decided by hanging pieces and missed one-move tactics — not opening
theory.** Knowledge for lessons is drawn from here.

## Knowledge

- [Video series: "Chess Fundamentals" — IM John Bartholomew (YouTube / lichess)](https://lichess.org/video/Ao9iOeK_jvU)
  Five short videos. #1 "Undefended Pieces" is the canonical lesson on building a
  sense of danger by scanning for loose pieces. Use for: blunder-checking, the
  pre-move scan, why loose pieces drop. The companion "Climbing the Rating Ladder"
  series shows the same habits applied in real beginner games.

- [Channel: John Bartholomew — "Resources for Chess Improvers"](https://johnbartholomewchess.com/chess/resources-for-chess-improvers/)
  IM Bartholomew's own curated improver path. Use for: deciding what to study next.

- [Article: "Best Free Chess Improvement Resources" — ChessGoals](https://chessgoals.com/best-free-chess-improvement-resources/)
  Comprehensive map of free tactics trainers and study tools. Use for: choosing
  where to do tactics (Lichess vs ChessTempo vs chess.com puzzles).

- [Article: "Best Training Plan for Chess Players Under 1000" — Chess Tournament Guide](https://chesstournamentguide.com/improve/best-training-plan-under-1000/)
  Diagnosis of *why* sub-1000 games are lost (hanging pieces, missed mates, late
  development, dead pieces) and a habit-first plan. Use for: setting weekly focus.

- [Study Plan: "Below 1000" — ChessMood](https://chessmood.com/chess-study-plans/for-beginners)
  Structured beginner curriculum. Use for: ordering of topics after tactics.

### Practice tools (where the reps happen)
- [Lichess Puzzles](https://lichess.org/training) — free, unlimited, drawn from real
  games. Primary tactics trainer. Use daily-ish, even 10 puzzles.
- [Lichess Practice](https://lichess.org/practice) — free interactive drills for
  basic checkmates (K+Q, K+R) and the opposition. Use for: endgame technique.
- [chess.com Puzzles](https://www.chess.com/puzzles) — 5/day free; fine if already
  on chess.com.

## Wisdom (Communities)
- [chess.com — "For Beginners" forum](https://www.chess.com/forum/category/help-support)
  Post an annotated loss and ask "where did this game turn?" Use for: getting real
  players to critique your decisions.
- [r/chessbeginners](https://www.reddit.com/r/chessbeginners/)
  High-traffic, friendly. Use for: "is this a good move/plan?" gut-checks and
  game reviews.
