# Starting point established: ~800 classical player, principles-first path agreed

Paul is ~800 on chess.com, plays classical/long time controls, and studies 1–3
hrs/week. He asked to learn "theory, openings, and middlegame," but accepted the
evidence-backed reframing that **tactics and blunder-checking dominate results below
~1300** — so the agreed sequence is tactics/blunder-checking → converting winning
positions → opening *principles* → middlegame plans, with memorised opening theory
explicitly deferred (see [[MISSION.md]]).

## Implications

- Because he plays classical, train *what to look for* over speed — he has the
  clock to use a checklist.
- No demonstrated-skill evidence yet; this record captures the disclosed starting
  point and the path, not a proven competency. Next floor will be set once he
